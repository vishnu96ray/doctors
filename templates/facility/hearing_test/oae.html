Types of otoacoustic emissions.

An Otoacoustic emission or OAE is a sound which is generated from within the inner ear.

Itâ€™s a low-level sound emitted by the cochlea either spontaneously or evoked by an auditory stimulus and it specifically provide information related to the function of the outer hair cells also known as OHC.

Broadly speaking, there are two types of otoacoustic emissions.

One it is known as Spontaneous Otoacoustic Emissions or say SOAEs, which can occur without external stimulation, and

Another is known as Evoked Otoacoustic Emissions or say EOAEs, which require an evoking stimulus.
